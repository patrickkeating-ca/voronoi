Fintech & Geometry

Two ideas, one geometric trick: nearest-seed classification.

Both explorations below use a Voronoi diagram — cells defined by which of a handful of seed points a location is closest to — as a visual and analytical device for fintech problems that are, underneath, just distance calculations.

No. 01

Robo-advisor model portfolios

Six model portfolios as seed points in (risk tolerance, time horizon) space; 320 synthetic clients classified to the nearest one. A market-regime overlay shifts every portfolio's risk coordinate at once and reports how many clients get reassigned — without answering a single new question.

No. 02

Market regime classification

Six market regimes as seeds over VIX × 10Y-2Y yield curve slope; every trading day since 2023 classified to the nearest one, using real FRED data. Seeds are draggable; the trail shows where the market has actually walked to get to today.
