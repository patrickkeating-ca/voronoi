Regimes & tilts
Regime transition matrix

P(next trading day's regime = column | today's regime = row), tallied from every day-over-day transition in the plotted history above.

Today's market sits in whichever regime it's closest to.

Characterize each historical market regime by two coordinates — equity volatility and the shape of the yield curve — and a day's readings can be classified by nearest neighbor, the same rule a robo-advisor uses to sort clients into model portfolios. The Voronoi cells are the regimes; the trail is where the market has actually walked to get to today.

The trail is the story

Each dot is one trading day, plotted by realized volatility and curve slope and colored by the regime it's nearest to. Connect them in order and you get a path — long loops sitting inside one cell are a regime holding; a path that crosses a boundary and doubles back is a false alarm the market shrugged off.

Today is a classification, not a label

The starred point is the most recent day. It didn't arrive with a regime name attached — it's simply nearest to one seed's coordinates. If it's sitting close to a boundary, the "current regime" call is fragile: a modest VIX print or a few basis points of curve steepening is enough to flip it.
